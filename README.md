# Purwadhika Capstone Project Module 2
## Explanatory Data Analysis for Airbnb Bangkok Dataset

## Overview
Bangkok is one of the world’s most visited cities, with an estimated 33-35 million annual visitors in 2026. Airbnb operates here as a dominant peer-to-peer lodging marketplace, bridging the gap between local Thai hospitality and global travel demand.

## Problem Statement
Starting in Q3 2026, we are initiating a massive, system-wide overhaul of the Airbnb guest experience. This initiative will reshape the entire user journey – from how potential guests discover and book listings on our app to their physical stay. To ensure these significant product changes drive user satisfaction and conversion, our new roadmap must be strictly data-driven and tailored to the reality of our current user base.

We are preparing to launch a major product and experience overhaul, but we need to validate our assumptions about our core users. "What are the defining characteristics, preferences, and booking behaviors of the guests who have stayed in our Bangkok listings over the past 12 months?" Without a comprehensive understanding of our recent guest profile, we risk designing features and experiences that misalign with what our actual market needs.

## Verdict and The Conclusion
The data reveals that Short-Stay bookings with Standard Pricing within Khlong Toei, Vadhana, and Ratchathewi represent the highest concentration of user demand and optimal market fit.

So, the recommendation would be:
- Frictionless Check-In/Out: Implement an express, automated check-in/out flow (e.g., smart locks, one-tap app checkout) that aligns with the fast-paced demands of short-stay guests.
- Integrated Visual Transit Guides: Embed hyper-local, visual public transport guides directly into the listing's itinerary to reduce navigation friction for highly mobile users.
- Seamless Airport Transfers: Introduce an add-on feature for direct airport pickups to guarantee a smooth, immediate transition from the terminal to the listing.
- "Welcome" Pantry Bundling: Offer an opt-in bundle for pre-stocked pantry essentials (local snacks, water, coffee) so guests can immediately settle in without an immediate grocery run.
